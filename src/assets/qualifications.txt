1. Supply Chain Practitioner
NQF Level 5 | SAQA ID 110942 | 180 Credits | 12 Months(SETA: TETA)
Course Overview
The Occupational Certificate: Supply Chain Practitioner equips learners with technical and operational skills required to coordinate and optimise activities across the end-to-end supply chain. This qualification prepares learners to manage procurement processes, logistics coordination, inventory systems, warehousing operations, and supplier relationships. It aligns with global supply chain management standards and integrates real workplace experience to enhance employability in logistics-intensive environments.
Learning Outcomes
Learners will be able to:
	•	Coordinate supply chain and logistics operations
	•	Manage procurement cycles and supplier relationships
	•	Optimise inventory and warehouse operations
	•	Analyse supply chain data for planning and decision-making
	•	Apply regulatory, compliance, and safety requirements
	•	Use ERP and digital systems to improve efficiency
A Qualified Learner Will Be Able To:
	•	Implement procurement strategies
	•	Manage stock levels and warehouse flow
	•	Coordinate transportation and distribution
	•	Ensure supply chain legal and safety compliance
	•	Support continuous improvement across the value chain
Entry Requirements
	•	NQF Level 4
	•	Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)
International Comparability
	•	UK: Diploma in Supply Chain Operations
	•	Australia: Diploma of Logistics
	•	New Zealand: Level 5 Supply Chain Management
Certification
Occupational Certificate: Supply Chain Practitioner (NQF 5)
Career Opportunities
	•	Logistics Coordinator
	•	Procurement Officer
	•	Inventory Controller
	•	Supply Chain Practitioner
	•	Warehouse Planner
Learning Options
Classroom • Blended • Workplace learning • EISA

2. Freight Handler
NQF Level 3 | SAQA ID 96396 | 122 Credits | 12 Months(SETA: TETA)
Course Overview
This qualification prepares learners to safely and efficiently handle freight across warehouse and transport operations. It covers the movement, storage, packing, securing, and documentation of cargo across courier, logistics, and freight industries. Safety and compliance form the core of this qualification.
Learning Outcomes
	•	Handle, move, and secure freight
	•	Follow health, safety, and environmental procedures
	•	Use Material Handling Equipment (MHE)
	•	Assist with warehouse receiving, dispatching, and storage
	•	Process freight labels and documentation
A Qualified Learner Will Be Able To:
	•	Prepare, pack, and secure loads
	•	Operate MHE safely
	•	Conduct stock counts and cycle counts
	•	Work within logistics teams
	•	Maintain accurate cargo records
Entry Requirements
	•	NQF Level 1 or RPL
International Comparability
	•	UK: Warehouse Operative Certificates
	•	Australia: Certificate II/III in Warehousing
	•	NZ: Level 3 Logistics Certificates
Certification
Occupational Certificate: Freight Handler (NQF 3)
Career Opportunities
	•	Freight Handler
	•	Warehouse Assistant
	•	Dispatch/Receiving Clerk
	•	Courier Operations Assistant
Learning Options
Classroom • Practical warehouse training • EISA
